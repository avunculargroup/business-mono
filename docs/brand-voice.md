## Company Identity

**Company name:** Bitcoin Treasury Solutions (BTS)

**Mission (preferred version):** "We make Bitcoin work for businesses: education, payments infrastructure, and treasury management from strategy to custody."

**Full scope:** Education, expert consultation, and implementation for merchant payments, B2B invoicing, vendor and salary payments, cross-border transfers, and strategic Bitcoin treasury reserves — including compliance, acquisition, and custody.

**Positioning:** Australia's dedicated, all-in-one Bitcoin business hub. Bitcoin-only focus (not "crypto"). Relationship-first. Globally connected for best-practice partnerships, deeply rooted in Australian regulations and needs.

**Key differentiators:**
- Personal, warm, human approach — genuine relationships, jargon-free guidance, ongoing support
- Full lifecycle coverage: advisor education → SME payment/treasury implementation → institutional strategies
- Central hub model: partner with global experts in custody, payments, compliance rather than building everything in-house
- Australia-specific: ATO, ASIC, local regulatory expertise

## Target Audience (Phased)

**Short term (immediate):** Accountants and lawyers who advise businesses and HNW clients. They face growing client questions about Bitcoin (payments, treasury, tax, compliance) and worry about giving poor advice or losing clients to more knowledgeable peers.

**Medium term:** The businesses and clients served by those advisors. SMEs losing money on card fees (2-3%), slow cross-border payments, FX costs. Need step-by-step guidance on Bitcoin payments and treasury.

**Long term:** Corporate treasuries, superannuation funds (including SMSFs), and Big 4 firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) who need specialised Bitcoin expertise for their own clients.

## Tone of Voice

**We sound:** Authoritative, Pragmatic, Warm

**We never sound:** Casual/sloppy, Condescending, Desperate

**Dinner party voice:** The quietly confident guest who really knows Bitcoin and business finance. Listens first, asks about your situation, then explains things clearly and practically. No jargon overload, no talking down, no hard sell — just straightforward, useful advice delivered with warmth and presence. Drops a dry, light-humour line occasionally ("Bitcoin's basically math that refuses to print more of itself, unlike some central banks") but only to connect, never to show off. By the end of the chat, you feel informed, capable, and reassured — not overwhelmed.

**Voice in one line:** Knowledgeable, capable, warm, present, always putting the client first.

## Bitcoin Stance & Key Arguments

### Core thesis (for sceptical CFOs)

Bitcoin functions as a resilient, primary treasury reserve asset — a scarce digital commodity with a fixed supply of 21 million units, verifiable, portable, and divisible — that fortifies institutional portfolios against systemic fragilities in traditional financial systems. Key framing:

- Holding excess cash beyond operational needs silently diminishes value through inflation (2-3% annual real purchasing power erosion)
- Protocol-enforced scarcity makes Bitcoin immune to fiat currency expansion
- Over 172 public companies globally hold Bitcoin on balance sheets as of Q3 2025
- Recommended allocation: 5-25% of reserves (scaled to risk tolerance), from excess cash beyond 2-5 month liquidity buffer
- Approach: dollar-cost averaging, no leverage, long-term hold, board-approved governance, hybrid custody (80-90%+ cold storage)
- Disposals only in existential scenarios
- Australian context: 32% crypto ownership, ASIC licensing accelerating, ATO treats as CGT asset

### Top 3 objections and responses

1. **Lack of regulatory clarity:** Guide through current landscape — 2025 Corporations Amendment Digital Assets Framework Bill, ATO CGT treatment (no GST on holdings), ASIC oversight. Provide tailored compliance roadmaps, tax advisor sign-offs, audit consultations, policy templates.

2. **No perceived benefit:** Demonstrate through real-world metrics — 172+ public companies holding BTC, MicroStrategy's growth, a 3% allocation running Monte Carlo simulations shows meaningful optionality with minimal downside amplification. Frame as inflation hedge and balance sheet resilience, not speculation.

3. **Volatility concerns:** Context over fear — Bitcoin's 70-80% drawdown cycles are real but recoveries have consistently set new highs. Frame volatility within long-term horizons (4+ year cycles). A modest 3% allocation barely moves portfolio volatility while adding significant upside optionality. No leverage, no trading, just strategic accumulation.

**Palette principle:** Warm, not cold. No pure white backgrounds or pure black text. Gold feels like a refined accent, not bitcoin-orange branding. Light mode by default.

**Typography:**
- Display/Headings: `Playfair Display` — serif, editorial, authoritative
- Body/UI: `DM Sans` — clean, geometric sans-serif
- Monospace (data): `JetBrains Mono` — bitcoin amounts, percentages, numerical data

**Aesthetic:** Premium asset manager meets modern software (Stripe/Linear polish). Warm off-white backgrounds, generous whitespace, gold accents used sparingly — they should feel earned, not sprayed. When in doubt: more whitespace, less colour, let the typography do the work.

**What it should never feel like:**
- Crypto-native or blockchain-hype (no neon, no dark mode by default, no rocket emojis)
- Generic SaaS template (no purple gradients)
- Overly corporate or stiff (no clip-art icons, no stock-photo energy)

### UI Microcopy Rules

When generating UI text, labels, empty states, or in-app copy:
- Plain, confident language. No jargon unless necessary, and explain it when used.
- No exclamation marks in UI copy.
- Action labels should be specific: "Review allocation strategy" not "Continue."
- Empty states should be helpful, not cutesy.
- No stock photos of bitcoin coins, physical gold, or generic finance imagery.
